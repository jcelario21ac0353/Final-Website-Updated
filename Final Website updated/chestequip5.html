Push-up

Push-ups are a fundamental bodyweight exercise that effectively target the chest muscles, specifically the pectoralis major, as well as the shoulders, triceps, and core. This versatile exercise can be performed anywhere, making it a staple in strength training routines.

How to do:

To perform a push-up, start in a high plank position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart and your feet together. Your body should form a straight line from your head to your heels. Engage your core and keep your gaze slightly ahead of you to maintain a neutral spine. Begin the movement by bending your elbows and lowering your body towards the ground in a controlled manner. Keep your elbows at a 45-degree angle to your body to reduce stress on your shoulders. Lower yourself until your chest nearly touches the floor. After reaching the bottom of the movement, exhale and push yourself back up to the starting position by extending your arms. Focus on squeezing your chest muscles as you press up. Repeat this motion for the desired number of repetitions. After completing your set, rest as needed before performing additional sets or other exercises.

DO's

  • Warm-up is essential before starting your push-up routine. This prepares your muscles and joints for the workout, reducing the risk of injury.
  • Always use proper form throughout the exercise. This involves maintaining correct posture, controlled movements, and appropriate hand placement. Proper form maximizes the effectiveness of the exercise and minimizes injury risk.
  • Gradually increase the number of push-ups you perform to allow your muscles and joints to adapt and grow stronger without undue stress.
  • Proper breathing is vital—inhale as you lower your body and exhale as you push back up. This helps maintain stability and control.
  • Perform the exercise with smooth, controlled motions, avoiding any jerking or bouncing. This ensures consistent muscle engagement and reduces the risk of injury.

DON'Ts

  • Avoid performing push-ups if you lack the necessary upper body strength. Attempting too many too soon can lead to injuries.
  • Never use momentum to push yourself up. This not only risks injury but also diminishes the exercise's effectiveness by relying on momentum rather than muscle strength.
  • Flaring your elbows out excessively can put undue stress on your shoulder joints. Keep your elbows at a 45-degree angle to your body to maintain proper alignment.
  • Do not sag your hips or arch your back excessively while performing push-ups. Maintain a neutral spine position to protect your spine and engage your core muscles.
  • Keep your feet stable throughout the exercise. Moving your feet or shifting your stance can decrease stability and compromise your form.
  • Never skip your warm-up. Warming up prepares your muscles for the workout, reducing the risk of strains and injuries.
